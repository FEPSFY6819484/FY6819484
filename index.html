          Abstract 
A Formula 1 cars aerodynamics can decide whether it stays on the track or takes a chance on taking off at speeds exceeding 200mph. Increasing a car's speed and efficiency has always been largely dependent on aerodynamics. Weather that’s the cigar-shaped models of the early days or complex, wind-tunnel-tested marvels of today, the development of aerodynamic innovation has transformed racing. This essay focuses on how aerodynamics in Formula 1 has evolved from very humble beginnings to its current position as a key shaper of performance, safety, and sustainability.
